Earthquake Triangulation Simulator

Description

To Play

  1. Press play, and watch the playhead move through the timeline.
  2. When it intersects with any of the markings, that marking's corresponding "location" will shake. This is representative of playing through the history of a recent earthquake - the only information you have is "at what time each location reported feeling the tremor"
  3. Hover over a location to reveal the exact time at which that location reported a tremor (in "seconds since the earthquake began")
  4. Click and drag out from a location to draw a radius around it. The number labelling the radius represents "seconds it would take a quake traveling from the radius to the location"
  5. Drag the radius out until the number labelling the radius matches the time-since-earthquake-began that said location reported feeling the tremor. Once these two numbers match, you have reduced the source of the quake to originating somewhere on the perimeter of this drawn circle.
  6. Repeat this process for the other two locations.
  7. Once you have two locations, you'll notice the only possible locations for the quake has reduced to 2 (non-identical circles can intersect at most at 2 points) Once you have three, there will be only one possible location left (that lands on the perimeter of all three radii)
  8. Click at the intersection of these circles
  9. You can then watch the simulated quake from that point. If you were very accurate with dragging out the circles, and clicking at their intersection, you should see the quake hit each location at exactly its reported time.(If not, don't worry about it. We can increase the allowed area for error later on).
  10. Try clicking on the perimeter of just one circle. See how the predicted time of intersection matches only for that location.
